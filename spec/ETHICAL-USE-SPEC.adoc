== Overview

This specification defines the normative requirements and guidance for the Ethical Use exhibit of the Palimpsest-MPL License.

== Definitions

Emotional Lineage::
The narrative, cultural, symbolic, and contextual meaning embedded in creative works.

Non-Interpretive System::
An automated system that processes content without preserving or considering its Emotional Lineage.

Ethical Use Declaration::
An acknowledgment that a user has read and understood this exhibit.

== Requirements

=== MUST (Normative)

1. **Metadata Preservation**
   - MUST NOT strip provenance metadata from distributed works
   - MUST preserve attribution information through processing pipelines

2. **Non-Interpretive System Notice**
   - MUST document use of covered works in AI/ML training
   - MUST NOT claim AI outputs carry original work's creative authority

3. **Commercial Acknowledgment**
   - Commercial users MUST acknowledge ethical use guidelines

=== SHOULD (Recommended)

1. **Emotional Lineage**
   - SHOULD preserve narrative context in derivative works
   - SHOULD acknowledge cultural heritage where documented

2. **Attribution**
   - SHOULD provide meaningful attribution beyond legal minimum
   - SHOULD link to original sources

3. **Community Support**
   - SHOULD contribute back to projects depended upon
   - SHOULD support communities whose works enable products

=== MAY (Optional)

1. **Enhanced Attribution**
   - MAY create LINEAGE.md documenting emotional context
   - MAY include cultural background in derivative works

2. **Community Engagement**
   - MAY contact original creators about significant derivative works
   - MAY seek guidance on culturally sensitive content

== Enforcement

=== Community Mechanisms

* Public statements by Stewardship Council
* Community awareness lists
* Guidance to creators on responding to misuse

=== Legal Remedies

Serious violations may be actionable under:

* Copyright infringement (if license terms violated)
* Contract law (if commercial agreement exists)
* Consumer protection law (if false claims made)

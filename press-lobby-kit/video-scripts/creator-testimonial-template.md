# Creator Testimonial Video Template

**Interview Framework for Palimpsest Licence Adopters**

---

## Overview

**Purpose:** Capture authentic stories from creators who have adopted the Palimpsest Licence

**Target Length:** 2-3 minutes per creator testimonial (can be compiled into a longer "Creator Stories" compilation)

**Format:** Interview-style with B-roll footage

**Tone:** Authentic, personal, empowering, diverse voices

**Output:** Individual testimonial videos + compiled montage version

---

## Creator Profiles (Diversity Matrix)

### Seek diverse representation across:

**Disciplines:**
- Writers (fiction, non-fiction, journalism, poetry)
- Visual artists (digital, traditional, photography)
- Musicians (composers, performers, producers)
- Developers (open-source contributors, game developers)
- Educators (academics, online course creators)
- Filmmakers/videographers

**Demographics:**
- Age: 18-70+ (wide range)
- Gender: Women, men, non-binary, gender-diverse
- Ethnicity: Global representation (particularly underrepresented communities)
- Geography: UK, EU, US, Asia, Africa, Latin America
- Career stage: Students, emerging creators, established professionals, retirees

**Experience Levels:**
- New to licensing (learning journey)
- Experienced with other licences (comparative insight)
- Legal/technical background (expert perspective)
- Non-technical creators (accessibility angle)

## Interview Format & Structure

### Pre-Interview Preparation

**Booking:**
- Email template: "We'd like to feature your story"
- 30-45 minute time commitment
- Consent form (video release, Palimpsest licensing of testimonial itself)
- Pre-interview questionnaire (background, experience with Palimpsest)

**Location:**
- In-person (preferred): Creator's workspace, studio, home office
- Remote: High-quality video call (Zoom, Riverside.fm for 4K recording)

**Equipment:**
- Camera: 4K minimum (DSLR, mirrorless, or high-end smartphone)
- Audio: Lavalier mic or shotgun mic (clear audio is critical)
- Lighting: Three-point lighting (key, fill, back) for in-person; ring light for remote

---

### Interview Structure (30-45 Minutes Total)

**Section 1: Introduction & Background (5-10 min)**
**Section 2: Why Palimpsest (10-15 min)**
**Section 3: Implementation Experience (5-10 min)**
**Section 4: Impact & Results (5-10 min)**
**Section 5: Advice & Vision (5 min)**

## Interview Questions (Complete List)

### SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION & BACKGROUND (5-10 minutes)

#### Q1.1: Who are you and what do you create?

**Prompt:**
> "Let's start with the basics. Tell me your name, where you're from, and what kind of creative work you do."

**Follow-up prompts:**
- How long have you been creating?
- What's your creative process like?
- Where do you share your work? (platforms, websites)

**Expected Answer Length:** 30-60 seconds

**B-Roll Notes:** Show creator's work (artwork, writing samples, code on screen, music playing)

---

#### Q1.2: How do you currently share your work online?

**Prompt:**
> "Walk me through how you share your work. What platforms do you use? Who's your audience?"

**Follow-up prompts:**
- GitHub, portfolio site, social media?
- How many people see your work?
- Do you sell your work, or is it freely shared?

**Expected Answer Length:** 45-60 seconds

**B-Roll Notes:** Screen recording of their website, GitHub profile, social media

---

#### Q1.3: What was your awareness of AI training on creative work?

**Prompt:**
> "Before you heard about Palimpsest, were you aware that AI systems like ChatGPT or Stable Diffusion might be training on your work?"

**Follow-up prompts:**
- When did you first realise this was happening?
- How did it make you feel?
- Did you try to do anything about it?

**Expected Answer Length:** 60-90 seconds

**B-Roll Notes:** News headlines about AI training, screenshots of AI-generated content

---

### SECTION 2: WHY PALIMPSEST (10-15 minutes)

#### Q2.1: How did you discover the Palimpsest Licence?

**Prompt:**
> "Tell me the story of how you first heard about Palimpsest. What was that moment like?"

**Follow-up prompts:**
- Where did you hear about it? (social media, article, friend)
- What was your initial reaction?
- What made you look into it further?

**Expected Answer Length:** 60-90 seconds

**B-Roll Notes:** Recreate discovery moment (scrolling Twitter, reading article, etc.)

---

#### Q2.2: What specific problem were you trying to solve?

**Prompt:**
> "What made you think, 'I need this licence'? Was there a specific incident or concern?"

**Follow-up prompts:**
- Had you seen your work used without permission?
- Were you worried about future AI developments?
- Was it more about principles than a specific threat?

**Expected Answer Length:** 90-120 seconds

**B-Roll Notes:** Visual representation of the problem (AI scraper graphic, news articles)

---

#### Q2.3: Why Palimpsest instead of other licences?

**Prompt:**
> "You could have used Creative Commons, MIT, or other licences. What made Palimpsest different?"

**Follow-up prompts:**
- Had you used other licences before?
- What was missing from those licences?
- What does Palimpsest offer that others don't?

**Expected Answer Length:** 90-120 seconds

**B-Roll Notes:** Comparison table (CC vs. MIT vs. Palimpsest)

---

#### Q2.4: What were your concerns or hesitations?

**Prompt:**
> "Was there anything that made you hesitate? Any concerns about adopting Palimpsest?"

**Follow-up prompts:**
- Did you worry it was too restrictive?
- Were you concerned about technical complexity?
- Did you worry about how others would react?

**Expected Answer Length:** 60-90 seconds

**B-Roll Notes:** Thoughtful creator (contemplative shots)

## B-Roll Shot List (Per Creator)

### Essential B-Roll (Every Interview)

**Workspace Shots (2-3 minutes footage):**
- Wide shot of creator's workspace (desk, studio, office)
- Close-up of tools (keyboard, paintbrush, camera, microphone)
- Creator working (typing, drawing, recording)
- Creator's hands in action (tactile, human element)

**Work Samples (2-3 minutes footage):**
- Examples of creator's work (with permission):
  - Writer: Pages of manuscript, blog posts
  - Artist: Paintings, digital art, photographs
  - Musician: Instruments, recording setup, sheet music
  - Developer: Code on screen, terminal commands, GitHub profile

**Lifestyle/Personality Shots (1-2 minutes footage):**
- Creator in natural setting (coffee shop, park, home)
- Candid moments (thinking, smiling, laughing)
- Personal items (books, art on walls, awards)

**Screen Recording (As applicable):**
- Creator's GitHub repository with Palimpsest licence
- Website/portfolio with licence notice
- Metadata in files
- Consent request interface (if applicable)

**Optional: Community/Audience Shots:**
- Creator at event (conference, meetup, gallery opening)
- Interactions with fans/followers (if comfortable)
- Social media engagement (comments, shares)

## Editing Guidelines

### Testimonial Edit (2-3 Minutes Per Creator)

**Structure:**
1. **Opening (0:00-0:15):** Name, discipline, location
   - "I'm Maya, a digital artist from London."
   - B-roll: Workspace, work samples

2. **Problem Statement (0:15-0:45):** Why they needed Palimpsest
   - "I discovered my artwork in Stable Diffusion's training set without permission."
   - B-roll: AI-generated art, news headlines

3. **Palimpsest Solution (0:45-1:30):** Implementation and experience
   - "Palimpsest gave me a way to say 'no' to AI training while keeping my work accessible."
   - B-roll: Adding licence to GitHub, website

4. **Impact (1:30-2:15):** Results and benefits
   - "Stability AI reached out, removed my work, and compensated me £500."
   - B-roll: Email correspondence, payment notification

5. **Call to Action (2:15-2:30):** Advice to other creators
   - "If you value your creative rights, Palimpsest is essential."
   - B-roll: Direct-to-camera, confident creator

**Pacing:**
- Keep it tight (remove ums, long pauses)
- Let emotional moments breathe (don't over-edit vulnerability)
- Balance talking head with B-roll (60% talking head, 40% B-roll)

**Music:**
- Subtle, unobtrusive background track
- Fades under voiceover, swells at transitions
- Genre: Inspirational, hopeful (matching creator's discipline if possible)

---

### Compilation Video (8-12 Minutes)

**Structure:**
- **Intro (0:00-0:30):** Title card "Creator Stories: Why Palimpsest Matters"
- **Theme 1: The Problem (0:30-2:30):** Multiple creators describe AI training concerns
- **Theme 2: Discovery (2:30-4:30):** How they found Palimpsest
- **Theme 3: Implementation (4:30-6:30):** Adding the licence
- **Theme 4: Impact (6:30-9:30):** Results, benefits, empowerment
- **Theme 5: The Future (9:30-11:30):** Vision for creative licensing
- **Outro (11:30-12:00):** Call to action, palimpsest.license

**Editing Technique:**
- Intercut multiple creators on same theme
- Create dialogue between perspectives (writer → artist → developer)
- Build narrative arc (problem → solution → hope)

## Production Notes

### Filming Setup (In-Person)

**Camera:**
- Primary: DSLR/mirrorless (Canon, Sony, Panasonic) - 4K 24fps or 30fps
- Secondary: B-roll camera or smartphone for cutaways

**Lenses:**
- Interview: 50mm or 85mm (shallow depth of field, flattering)
- B-roll: 24-70mm zoom (versatile)

**Lighting:**
- Key light: Softbox or LED panel (45° angle, eye level)
- Fill light: Reflector or second LED (opposite side, lower intensity)
- Back light: Hair light (separates subject from background)

**Audio:**
- Primary: Lavalier mic (wired or wireless, clipped to shirt)
- Backup: Shotgun mic on camera (in case lav fails)
- Room tone: Record 30 seconds for editing

**Background:**
- Creator's natural workspace (authentic)
- Depth: Position creator 3-6 feet from background (avoid flat look)
- Tidy but lived-in (not sterile)

---

### Remote Recording Setup

**Platform:** Riverside.fm or Zoom (high-quality recording modes)

**Creator Guidance (Pre-Interview Email):**
- **Camera:** Position at eye level (stack books under laptop if needed)
- **Lighting:** Face a window OR use desk lamp in front of you
- **Audio:** Use headphones (reduces echo), speak clearly
- **Background:** Tidy, non-distracting space
- **Internet:** Wired connection if possible, close other applications
- **Test Call:** 15 minutes before interview (check setup)

**Recording Settings:**
- Local recording (both sides record independently, sync in post)
- 1080p minimum, 4K if possible
- Separate audio tracks

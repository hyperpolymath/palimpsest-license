# Legal Citation Guide
## Multi-Jurisdictional Reference Scheme for Palimpsest License

**Purpose:** Standardised citation formats for UK, Scotland, Netherlands, and EU legal sources
**Style Standards:** OSCOLA (UK/Scotland), ECLI (Netherlands/EU), Bluebook (comparative reference)
**Last Updated:** November 2025

---

## 1. United Kingdom Citation Formats

### 1.1 OSCOLA (Oxford University Standard for Citation of Legal Authorities)

**OSCOLA 4th Edition** is the standard citation system for UK legal materials.

#### 1.1.1 Case Law

**Format:** *Case Name* [Year] Court Abbreviation Number

**Examples:**
```
Infopaq International A/S v Danske Dagblades Forening [2009] EUECJ C-5/08
Newspaper Licensing Agency Ltd v Meltwater Holding BV [2011] EWCA Civ 890
Ashdown v Telegraph Group Ltd [2001] EWCA Civ 1142
```

**Neutral Citations (Post-2001):**
- **England and Wales High Court:** [Year] EWHC Number (Division)
  - Example: *Twentieth Century Fox Film Corp v Newzbin Ltd* [2010] EWHC 608 (Ch)
  - Divisions: (Ch) Chancery, (QB) Queen's Bench, (Fam) Family, (Admin) Administrative, (Comm) Commercial, (TCC) Technology and Construction

- **England and Wales Court of Appeal:** [Year] EWCA Civ/Crim Number
  - Example: *Sawkins v Hyperion Records Ltd* [2005] EWCA Civ 565

- **UK Supreme Court:** [Year] UKSC Number
  - Example: *Newspaper Licensing Agency Ltd v Marks & Spencer plc* [2001] UKHL 38
  - Note: Pre-2009 Supreme Court cases cited as UKHL (House of Lords)

- **Intellectual Property Enterprise Court (IPEC):** [Year] EWHC Number (IPEC)
  - Example: *Phonographic Performance Ltd v Hagan* [2016] EWHC 3076 (IPEC)

**Law Reports (Pre-Neutral Citation):**
```
CBS Songs Ltd v Amstrad Consumer Electronics plc [1988] AC 1013
Clark v Associated Newspapers Ltd [1998] 1 WLR 1558
Pasterfield v Denham [1999] FSR 168
```

**Abbreviations:**
- AC = Appeal Cases (House of Lords/Supreme Court)
- WLR = Weekly Law Reports
- FSR = Fleet Street Reports (IP cases)
- EWCA = England and Wales Court of Appeal
- EWHC = England and Wales High Court

#### 1.1.2 Legislation

**Acts of Parliament:**
```
Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988
Intellectual Property Act 2014
Data Protection Act 2018
```

**Section References:**
```
CDPA 1988, s 77
CDPA 1988, ss 12-15
Data Protection Act 2018, Sch 2, para 26
```

**Statutory Instruments:**
```
Copyright and Related Rights Regulations 2003 (SI 2003/2498)
Copyright and Rights in Performances (Quotation and Parody) Regulations 2014 (SI 2014/2356)
```

#### 1.1.3 Books and Articles

**Books:**
```
Hector L MacQueen and Charlotte Waelde, Intellectual Property Law in Scotland (W Green 2007)
Hugh Laddie et al, The Modern Law of Copyright and Designs (4th edn, Butterworths 2011)
```

**Journal Articles:**
```
Mira T Sundara Rajan, 'Moral Rights in the Digital Age' (2004) 24 OJLS 233
```

#### 1.1.4 Online Resources

**Legislation:**
```
Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 <https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1988/48> accessed 22 November 2025
```

**Official Guidance:**
```
UK Intellectual Property Office, 'Exceptions to Copyright' (2014) <https://www.gov.uk/guidance/exceptions-to-copyright> accessed 22 November 2025
```

## 2. Scottish Citation Formats

### 2.1 Scottish Courts Neutral Citation

**Format:** [Year] Court Abbreviation Number

#### 2.1.1 Court of Session

**Outer House (First Instance):**
```
[Year] CSOH Number

Example: Mars UK Ltd v Teknowledge Ltd [2000] FSR 138 (CSOH)
```

**Inner House (Appeal):**
```
[Year] CSIH Number

Example: Grierson v Grierson [1993] SC 259 (CSIH)
```

#### 2.1.2 Sheriff Court

**Format:**
```
[Year] SC{Location Abbreviation} Number

Examples:
[2023] SCEDIN 45 (Sheriff Court Edinburgh)
[2023] SCGLA 123 (Sheriff Court Glasgow)
```

**Location Abbreviations:**
- EDIN = Edinburgh
- GLA = Glasgow
- ABE = Aberdeen
- DUN = Dundee
- INVS = Inverness

#### 2.1.3 Scottish Appeals to UK Supreme Court

```
[Year] UKSC Number

Example: Newspaper Licensing Agency Ltd v Marks & Spencer plc [2003] 1 AC 551 (HL) (Scottish appeal)
```

**Note:** Indicate "(Scottish appeal)" or "(Sc)" when relevant to distinguish from English appeals.

### 2.2 Scottish Institutional Writers

**Citation Format:** Author, *Title* [Book].[Title].[Section]

**Examples:**
```
Stair, Institutions I.9.4
Erskine, Institute III.3.10
Bell, Principles § 1381
```

**Full Citations:**
```
Viscount Stair, Institutions of the Law of Scotland (1681) I.9.4
John Erskine, Institute of the Law of Scotland (1773) III.3.10
George Joseph Bell, Principles of the Law of Scotland (10th edn, 1899) § 1381
```

### 2.3 Scottish Legislation

**Acts of the Scottish Parliament:**
```
Gaelic Language (Scotland) Act 2005
Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995
Arbitration (Scotland) Act 2010
```

**Section References:**
```
Requirements of Writing (Scotland) Act 1995, s 1
Scotland Act 1998, Sch 5, Pt II, s C2
```

## 3. Netherlands Citation Formats

### 3.1 ECLI (European Case Law Identifier)

**Standard Format:** ECLI:NL:[Court]:[Year]:[Number]

#### 3.1.1 Hoge Raad (Supreme Court)

```
ECLI:NL:HR:[Year]:[Number]

Examples:
ECLI:NL:HR:1991:ZC0109 (Van Dale v Romme)
ECLI:NL:HR:2002:AE7680 (Scientology)
ECLI:NL:HR:2001:AA9374 (Endstra Tapes)
```

**Full Citation:**
```
Hoge Raad, 4 January 1991, ECLI:NL:HR:1991:ZC0109 (Van Dale v Romme)
HR 29 November 2002, ECLI:NL:HR:2002:AE7680 (Scientology)
```

#### 3.1.2 Gerechtshof (Court of Appeal)

**Format:** ECLI:NL:GH[Location]:[Year]:[Number]

**Court Abbreviations:**
- GHAMS = Gerechtshof Amsterdam
- GHDHA = Gerechtshof Den Haag (The Hague)
- GHSHE = Gerechtshof 's-Hertogenbosch
- GHARL = Gerechtshof Arnhem-Leeuwarden

**Examples:**
```
ECLI:NL:GHAMS:2010:BM9157
Gerechtshof Amsterdam, 24 June 2010, ECLI:NL:GHAMS:2010:BM9157 (Eyeworks)
```

#### 3.1.3 Rechtbank (District Court)

**Format:** ECLI:NL:RB[Location]:[Year]:[Number]

**Court Abbreviations:**
- RBDHA = Rechtbank Den Haag
- RBAMS = Rechtbank Amsterdam
- RBROT = Rechtbank Rotterdam
- RBGEL = Rechtbank Gelderland

**Examples:**
```
ECLI:NL:RBDHA:2020:5432
Rechtbank Den Haag, 15 March 2020, ECLI:NL:RBDHA:2020:5432
```

### 3.2 Dutch Legislation

**Auteurswet (Copyright Act):**
```
Auteurswet 1912 (Aw)
Aw, art 10
Aw, art 25(1)(a)-(d)
```

**Full Format:**
```
Auteurswet 1912, Staatsblad 1912, 308 (as amended)
```

**Online Reference:**
```
Auteurswet 1912 <https://wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0001886> accessed 22 November 2025
```

**Civil Code:**
```
Burgerlijk Wetboek (BW)
BW Boek 6, art 248 (Book 6, Article 248)
BW 6:248
```

### 3.3 Academic Commentary (Dutch)

**Books:**
```
J.H. Spoor, D.W.F. Verkade, D.J.G. Visser, Auteursrecht (Wolters Kluwer 2019)
P.B. Hugenholtz & A.A. Quaedvlieg, Auteursrecht op Maat (Amsterdam University Press 2021)
```

**Articles:**
```
P.B. Hugenholtz, 'The New Copyright Directive: Text and Data Mining (Articles 3 and 4)' (2019) Kluwer Copyright Blog
M.H.M. Schellekens, 'Smart Contracts and the Law' (2018) Computerrecht 234
```

### 3.4 Dutch Short Form Citations

**Case Law:**
```
HR 4 January 1991 (Van Dale)
Hof Amsterdam 24 June 2010 (Eyeworks)
Rb. Den Haag 15 March 2020
```

**Note:** Short form acceptable after first full citation.

## 4. European Union Citation Formats

### 4.1 Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU)

**ECLI Format:** ECLI:EU:C:[Year]:[Number]

**Examples:**
```
ECLI:EU:C:2009:465
Case C-5/08 Infopaq International A/S v Danske Dagblades Forening [2009] ECLI:EU:C:2009:465
```

**Traditional Format:**
```
Case C-476/17 Pelham GmbH v Hütter [2019] ECLI:EU:C:2019:624
Joined Cases C-293/12 and C-594/12 Digital Rights Ireland Ltd [2014] ECLI:EU:C:2014:238
```

**General Court:**
```
Case T-123/18 [Year] ECLI:EU:T:[Year]:[Number]
```

### 4.2 EU Legislation

**Directives:**
```
Directive 2001/29/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 22 May 2001 on the harmonisation of certain aspects of copyright and related rights in the information society [2001] OJ L167/10 (Information Society Directive)

Short form: InfoSoc Directive, Directive 2001/29/EC

Directive 2019/790/EU on copyright and related rights in the Digital Single Market [2019] OJ L130/92 (DSM Directive)
```

**Regulations:**
```
Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data [2016] OJ L119/1 (GDPR)

Short form: GDPR, Regulation (EU) 2016/679
```

**Article References:**
```
GDPR, art 6(1)(f)
InfoSoc Directive, art 5(3)
DSM Directive, recital 15
```

### 4.3 EU Advocates General Opinions

```
Opinion of Advocate General Szpunar in Case C-476/17 Pelham GmbH v Hütter ECLI:EU:C:2018:1002

Short form: AG Szpunar in Pelham
```

## 7. Cross-Referencing Between Jurisdictions

### 7.1 Parallel Citations

**When a Case Cited in Multiple Jurisdictions:**

**Example (Infopaq in UK, EU, and Netherlands):**
```
First citation:
Infopaq International A/S v Danske Dagblades Forening, Case C-5/08 [2009] ECLI:EU:C:2009:465; [2009] EUECJ C-5/08 (UK neutral citation); cited in HR 4 January 1991, ECLI:NL:HR:1991:ZC0109

Subsequent citations:
Infopaq (CJEU)
Infopaq [2009] EUECJ C-5/08 (UK format)
```

### 7.2 Translation of Foreign Citations

**When Citing Translated Judgments:**
```
Hoge Raad, 4 January 1991, ECLI:NL:HR:1991:ZC0109 (Van Dale v Romme) [translated by author]

OR

HR 4 January 1991 (Van Dale) (unofficial translation available at [URL])
```

## 8. Palimpsest-Specific Citation Conventions

### 8.1 Licence Clause References

**Format:** Palimpsest License v[Version], cl [Clause Number]

**Examples:**
```
Palimpsest License v0.4, cl 1.2 (Non-Interpretive Systems Consent Requirement)
Palimpsest License v0.4, cl 2.3 (Metadata Preservation)
```

**Short Form (After First Citation):**
```
First: Palimpsest License v0.4, cl 1.2
Subsequent: Palimpsest cl 1.2; cl 1.2
```

### 8.2 Palimpsest Governance Documents

**Format:**
```
Palimpsest Stewardship Council, [Document Title] ([Version/Date])

Examples:
Palimpsest Stewardship Council, Governance Model v1.0 (November 2025)
Palimpsest Stewardship Council, 'Emotional Lineage: Interpretive Framework' (2025)
```

### 8.3 Bilingual Citations (Dutch-English)

**When Citing Palimpsest License in Both Languages:**
```
Palimpsest License v0.4 (English version) / Palimpsest Licentie v0.4 (Nederlandse versie)

Clause reference with bilingual alignment:
Palimpsest License v0.4, cl 1.2 (English) / Palimpsest Licentie v0.4, art 1.2 (Nederlands)
```

**Note:** Specify which version prevails in legal disputes (Dutch in Netherlands jurisdiction).

## 11. Style Guide for Palimpsest Documentation

### 11.1 General Principles

**Consistency:**
- Use same citation style throughout document
- First full citation, then short form
- Italicise case names, legislation not italicised

**Clarity:**
- Provide sufficient information for reader to locate source
- Include access dates for online sources
- Specify translation status for non-English sources

### 11.2 Footnotes vs In-Text Citations

**Legal Academic Style (Preferred):**
- Footnotes for citations
- Substantive discussion in main text
- Cross-references in footnotes

**Example:**
```
Main text:
The UK Court of Appeal held that the hypothetical licence fee approach was appropriate.¹

Footnote:
¹ Newspaper Licensing Agency Ltd v Meltwater Holding BV [2011] EWCA Civ 890 [49]-[52].
```

**Inline Style (For Web Documentation):**
```
The UK Court of Appeal in *Newspaper Licensing Agency Ltd v Meltwater* [2011] EWCA Civ 890 held that the hypothetical licence fee approach was appropriate.
```

### 11.3 Pinpoint References

**Paragraphs:**
```
Infopaq [2009] EUECJ C-5/08 [37]
Ashdown [2001] EWCA Civ 1142 [43]-[45]
```

**Pages (Older Reports):**
```
CBS Songs [1988] AC 1013, 1054
```

**Articles (Dutch Law):**
```
Aw, art 25(1)(a)
BW, art 6:248
```

**Sections (UK Statutes):**
```
CDPA 1988, s 77(1)-(3)
CDPA 1988, ss 12-15
```

## 14. Conclusion

This citation guide provides standardised formats for multi-jurisdictional legal referencing in Palimpsest License documentation. Consistency and clarity are paramount for international accessibility and legal precision.

**Key Takeaways:**
1. **UK/Scotland:** Use OSCOLA with neutral citations
2. **Netherlands:** Use ECLI format with Dutch abbreviations
3. **EU:** Use ECLI and traditional Case C-X/YY format
4. **Cross-jurisdictional:** Provide parallel citations and translation notes
5. **Palimpsest-specific:** Bilingual clause references with version numbers

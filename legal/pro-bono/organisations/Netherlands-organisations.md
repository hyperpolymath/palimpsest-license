# Pro Bono Legal Organisations - Netherlands

## Overview
The Netherlands is the **primary legal jurisdiction** for the Palimpsest License (Dutch law governs). This makes Dutch legal expertise absolutely critical for the project's legitimacy, enforceability, and alignment with European copyright frameworks.

**Legal Context:** The license must comply with Auteurswet (Dutch Copyright Act), Dutch Civil Code (Burgerlijk Wetboek), and EU directives (InfoSoc, DSM Directive, AI Act).

## Dutch Legal System Considerations

### Key Legal Framework
1. **Auteurswet (Copyright Act 1912, as amended):** Governs all copyright in NL
2. **Burgerlijk Wetboek (Civil Code):** Contract law, licensing agreements
3. **EU Directives:** InfoSoc Directive (2001/29/EC), DSM Directive (2019/790)
4. **Moral Rights:** Strong protection under Dutch law (Article 25 Auteurswet)
5. **AI Act (EU Regulation 2024/1689):** Will apply in Netherlands from 2025/2026

### Critical Dutch Law Questions for Experts
1. **Auteurswet Article 25 compliance:** Moral rights (paternity, integrity) in Palimpsest License
2. **Auteurswet Article 45c-h:** Exception for text and data mining (TDM) - how to opt-out for AI training
3. **DSM Directive Articles 3-6:** Press publishers' rights, out-of-commerce works
4. **Contract law validity:** Are Palimpsest's "non-interpretive consent" clauses enforceable?
5. **Stichting structure:** Should Palimpsest Council be a Stichting (foundation) under Dutch law?
6. **Collective rights management:** Any overlap with collective management organisations?

### Dutch Language Requirements
- **Legal binding version MUST be in Dutch** for Dutch law enforceability
- Professional legal translation essential (not machine translation)
- Both Dutch and English versions needed for accessibility
- Consider certified translator for legal precision

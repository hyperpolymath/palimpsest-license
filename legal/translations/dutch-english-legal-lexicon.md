# Dutch-English Legal Lexicon
## Bilingual Legal Terminology for Palimpsest License

**Purpose:** Standardised legal translation guide for Dutch-English terminology
**Primary Use:** Palimpsest License bilingual framework (Dutch legally binding, English informative)
**Translation Standard:** Functional equivalence with legal precision
**Last Updated:** November 2025

---

## 1. Translation Principles

### 1.1 Legal Translation Methodology

**Functional Equivalence:**
- Translate legal effect, not merely words
- Identify equivalent legal concepts between systems
- Acknowledge where no direct equivalent exists

**Harmonised EU Terminology:**
- Use standardised EU English-Dutch terminology where applicable
- EUR-Lex multilingual glossary as reference
- Consistency with EU Directives and CJEU judgments

**Legal Precision:**
- Avoid colloquialisms and ambiguity
- Maintain formal register (legal language)
- Preserve structural parallelism between versions

### 1.2 Translation Challenges: Civil Law vs Common Law

**Conceptual Differences:**
- Dutch law = civil law (codified, systematic)
- English law = common law (case law-based)
- Scottish law = mixed (civil + common law)

**Implications:**
- Some Dutch legal concepts have no English common law equivalent
- Translation requires explanation, not mere word substitution
- Contextual notes necessary for non-equivalent concepts

### 1.3 Palimpsest Bilingual Framework

**Dutch Version (Primary):**
- Legally binding in Netherlands (primary jurisdiction)
- Authentic legal text for Dutch courts
- Formal legal Dutch terminology

**English Version (Informative):**
- International accessibility
- Not legally binding in Netherlands unless parties agree
- British English spelling and grammar (not US English)

**Alignment Methodology:**
- Clause-by-clause structural parallelism
- Numbered clauses correspond exactly
- Cross-reference table in `docs/bilingual-map.md`

## 11. Translation Challenges and Solutions

### 11.1 False Friends (Valse Vrienden)

**Terms that Look Similar but Differ in Meaning:**

| **Nederlands** | **Literal English** | **Actual Legal Meaning** | **Notes** |
|----------------|---------------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| **openbaarmakingsrecht** | "public-making right" | right of publication, right of communication to the public | Not merely "making public" |
| **eventueel** | "eventual" | possibly, if applicable | NOT "eventual" (which = *uiteindelijk*) |
| **actueel** | "actual" | current, topical | NOT "actual" (which = *werkelijk*) |
| **consequent** | "consequent" | consistent | NOT "as a consequence" (which = *bijgevolg*) |

### 11.2 Untranslatable Concepts

**Dutch Concepts with No Direct English Equivalent:**

| **Nederlands** | **Explanation** | **Translation Strategy** |
|----------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| **kort geding** | Fast-track preliminary relief proceedings before *voorzieningenrechter* | Translate as "summary proceedings" or "preliminary relief proceedings"; add explanatory note |
| **redelijkheid en billijkheid** | Reasonableness and fairness (BW 6:248); overarching principle of good faith in contracts | Translate as "reasonableness and fairness" or "good faith and fair dealing"; contextualise with civil law tradition |
| **nobile officium** (Scots law parallel) | Equitable jurisdiction of Court of Session | No Dutch equivalent; explain as equitable power |

### 11.3 British English vs Dutch Legal Culture

**Register and Formality:**
- Dutch legal language: formal, technical, precise
- British legal English: formal but may use Latin phrases (*inter alia*, *inter se*, *mutatis mutandis*)
- Consistency: use same level of formality in both versions

**Structural Differences:**
- Dutch: often uses compound nouns (*auteursrechtinbreuk* = copyright + infringement)
- English: separate words or hyphenated (*copyright infringement*)

## 12. Translation Workflow for Palimpsest

### 12.1 Translation Process

**Step 1: Draft Dutch Version**
- Primary legal text
- Reviewed by Dutch legal experts
- Approved by Palimpsest Stewardship Council

**Step 2: Translate to English**
- Professional legal translator (Dutch to British English)
- Functional equivalence approach
- Maintain clause numbering and structure

**Step 3: Legal Review**
- UK/Scottish legal expert reviews English version
- Ensure concepts align with British legal terminology
- Identify where Dutch concept has no English equivalent

**Step 4: Alignment Check**
- Compare clause-by-clause
- Create bilingual map (`docs/bilingual-map.md`)
- Verify structural parallelism

**Step 5: Community Review**
- Public consultation on both versions
- Cultural sensitivity review
- Accessibility check (plain language options)

### 12.2 Maintaining Bilingual Alignment

**Versioning:**
- Both versions must have same version number (e.g., v0.4)
- Any amendment to Dutch version requires corresponding English amendment
- Changelog documents both versions

**Change Control:**
- Amendments tracked in version control (Git)
- Bilingual amendment process (simultaneous)
- Council approval required for both versions

### 12.3 Dispute Resolution Clause

**Language Precedence:**
```
Dutch Version Prevails in Netherlands Jurisdiction:

In the event of any discrepancy between the Dutch and English versions of this licence, the Dutch version shall prevail in proceedings before Dutch courts. In proceedings before courts of other jurisdictions, the version in the language of that jurisdiction (if available) or the English version shall be used, with reference to the Dutch version for interpretation.

Nederlandse Versie Prevaleert in Nederlandse Jurisdictie:

In het geval van enige discrepantie tussen de Nederlandse en Engelse versies van deze licentie, prevaleert de Nederlandse versie in procedures voor Nederlandse rechtbanken. In procedures voor rechtbanken van andere jurisdicties wordt de versie in de taal van die jurisdictie (indien beschikbaar) of de Engelse versie gebruikt, met verwijzing naar de Nederlandse versie voor interpretatie.
```

**ISO 17100:2015 (Translation Services):**

- Quality assurance measures

## 15. Conclusion

This bilingual lexicon provides standardised Dutch-English legal terminology for the Palimpsest License framework. It ensures consistency, legal precision, and accessibility across both language versions.

1. **Functional equivalence:** Legal effect, not literal translation

# Honest Broker Candidates for Palimpsest License

## Overview

"Honest brokers" are independent, respected experts who can provide impartial guidance, validate legal frameworks, and lend credibility to the Palimpsest License project. They serve as trusted advisors, mediators in disputes, and authoritative voices in policy debates.

**Selection Criteria:**
- ✅ Recognised expertise in IP law, technology law, or AI ethics
- ✅ Reputation for independence and integrity
- ✅ No conflicts of interest (not representing AI companies, platforms with adverse interests)
- ✅ Respected by multiple stakeholder groups (creators, technologists, policymakers)
- ✅ Track record of public interest work or balanced scholarship
- ✅ Willingness to engage with innovative/unconventional legal frameworks

**Roles for Honest Brokers:**
1. **Legal Validation:** Review and endorse license structure
2. **Dispute Resolution:** Serve as mediator/arbitrator in license disputes
3. **Policy Advocacy:** Lend authority to policy submissions and testimony
4. **Advisory Council:** Provide ongoing strategic guidance
5. **Public Credibility:** Association with respected figures enhances legitimacy

## Engagement Strategy for Honest Brokers

### Principles

**1. Approach with Humility**
- These are respected authorities; we're asking for their expertise
- Emphasise learning, not advocacy
- Welcome critical feedback, not seeking rubber stamps

**2. Offer Genuine Value**
- Research opportunities (novel legal questions)
- Policy impact (influence real-world outcomes)
- Intellectual engagement (stimulating legal/ethical questions)
- Professional credit (acknowledgment, co-authorship)

**3. Respect Time Constraints**
- Busy, high-profile individuals
- Be specific about asks (hours, deliverables)
- Offer flexibility (timelines, scope)
- Compensate if appropriate (honoraria)

**4. Leverage Networks**
- Warm introductions better than cold emails
- Academic networks, professional associations
- Shared contacts (Creative Commons, EFF, etc.)

**5. Build Relationships Over Time**
- Not transactional (don't ask for endorsement immediately)
- Intellectual exchange first
- Ongoing engagement, not one-off requests

### Advisory Council Structure (Proposed)

**Palimpsest Advisory Council** (distinct from Stewardship Council)

**Composition (5-7 members):**
- 2 Academic legal scholars (e.g., Hugenholtz, Samuelson or Edwards)
- 1 Digital rights/AI ethics expert (e.g., Cohn, Gebru, or Whittaker)
- 1 International/cultural rights expert (e.g., Shaheed or Chander)
- 1 Judicial/practitioner (e.g., retired judge or senior lawyer)
- 1-2 Public intellectuals/creators (e.g., Doctorow, or prominent creator)

**Role:**
- Provide expert guidance (not day-to-day governance)
- Validate major decisions (license revisions, test cases)
- Lend credibility and public profile
- Mediate disputes if needed
- Advise on policy strategy

**Commitment:**
- Quarterly meetings (virtual)
- Review major proposals (1-2 hours/quarter)
- Optional: public statements, policy testimony

**Compensation:**
- Honorarium (small, symbolic - e.g., €500-1000/year)
- Expense reimbursement (travel if in-person events)
- Professional acknowledgment (prominent credit in materials)

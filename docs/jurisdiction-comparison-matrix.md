## 3. Moral Rights Detailed Comparison

### 3.1 Statutory Basis

| **Jurisdiction** | **Paternity** | **Integrity** | **Disclosure** | **Privacy** | **Inalienability** |
|------------------|---------------|---------------|----------------|-------------|--------------------|
| **UK** | CDPA s 77 (assertion required) | CDPA s 80 (derogatory treatment) | No statutory right | CDPA s 85 (photos/films) | Cannot assign (s 94); waivable (s 87) |
| **Scotland** | CDPA s 77 (Scots interpretation) | CDPA s 80 (civilian-friendly) | No statutory right | CDPA s 85 | Same as UK; civilian tradition supports |
| **Netherlands** | Aw Art. 25(1)(b) (automatic) | Aw Art. 25(1)(c)-(d) (two-part test) | Aw Art. 25(1)(a) (first publication) | No specific right | **Strictly inalienable** (Art. 25(3)); specific waiver only (Art. 25(5)) |
| **EU** | InfoSoc recital 20 (minimum harmonisation) | InfoSoc recital 19 (Member State discretion) | Not harmonised | Not harmonised | Member State discretion |

### 3.2 Paternity Right Specifics

**UK (CDPA s 77):**
- **Assertion requirement:** Must be asserted in writing, signed by author (s 78)
- **Identification:** "Reasonably prominent" (s 77(7)-(8))
- **Exceptions:** Computer programmes, typefaces, computer-generated works, employees (certain cases)

**Netherlands (Aw Art. 25(1)(b)):**
- **Automatic:** No assertion required
- **Scope:** Right to be named as author, use pseudonym, or remain anonymous
- **Robust:** Cannot be waived in advance (Art. 25(5)); only specific instances

**Palimpsest Alignment:**
- ✓ **Netherlands superior:** Automatic attribution, inalienable
- ✓ **Clause 2.3 enhances:** Metadata preservation beyond name (emotional lineage markers)

### 3.3 Integrity Right Comparison

| **Element** | **UK (s 80)** | **Netherlands (Art. 25(1)(c)-(d))** | **Palimpsest Requirement** |
|-------------|---------------|--------------------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Test** | "Derogatory treatment" = distortion, mutilation, prejudicial to honour/reputation | (c) Any modification (unless unreasonable to object)<br>(d) Derogatory treatment to honour/work | Contextual integrity + emotional lineage preservation |
| **Standard** | Objective (reasonable person) | (c) Reasonableness test<br>(d) Prejudice to honour | Subjective (author) + community standards |
| **Scope** | Addition, deletion, alteration, adaptation | Same + broader protection | Includes metadata, symbolic texture, cultural context |
| **Waiver** | Permitted (s 87) | Specific waiver only (Art. 25(5)) | Difficult (ethical accountability persists) |

**Case Law Examples:**

**UK:**
- *Tidy v Natural History Museum* [1996]: Objective test; claimant must prove prejudice
- *Pasterfield v Denham* [1999]: Honour as author, not general reputation
- *Confetti Records* [2003]: Unauthorised sampling may engage integrity right

**Netherlands:**
- HR 12 January 2001 (Endstra Tapes): Context of publication matters
- Hof Amsterdam 24 June 2010 (Eyeworks): Format changes may violate integrity

**Palimpsest Advantage in Netherlands:**
- Stronger protection against modifications
- Inalienable (cannot be contracted away)
- Cultural context preservation aligns with Dutch legal values

## 8. Jurisdictional Choice and Conflict of Laws

### 8.1 Choice of Law (Contractual Obligations)

**Rome I Regulation (EU) No 593/2008 (retained in UK post-Brexit):**

| **Principle** | **Application to Palimpsest** |
|---------------|-------------------------------|
| **Party Autonomy (Art. 3)** | Parties may choose governing law → **Netherlands law chosen for Palimpsest** |
| **Default Rule (Art. 4)** | If no choice: law of habitual residence of service provider (licensor) |
| **Mandatory Rules Override (Art. 9)** | Mandatory consumer protection, competition law may override choice |
| **Public Policy (Art. 21)** | Choice may be refused if contrary to public policy of forum |

**Palimpsest Choice of Netherlands Law:**
- ✓ Valid under Rome I Art. 3
- ✓ UK/Scottish courts will apply Dutch law if Palimpsest dispute litigated there
- ✓ Mandatory UK law (e.g., consumer protection) may override in specific contexts

### 8.2 Jurisdiction (Court Selection)

**Brussels Ibis Regulation (EU) No 1215/2012 (retained in UK with modifications):**

| **Principle** | **Application** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Domicile Rule (Art. 4)** | Defendant sued in courts of domicile |
| **Special Jurisdiction (Art. 7(2))** | Copyright infringement: place where harmful event occurred (dual localisation) |
| **Choice of Court (Art. 25)** | Parties may agree exclusive jurisdiction (must be in writing) |

**Palimpsest Jurisdiction Options:**
1. **Netherlands (primary):** Dutch courts apply Dutch law (Auteurswet)
2. **Scotland:** Scottish courts apply Dutch law (if choice-of-law clause invoked) or Scots law (if infringement localised in Scotland)

**Hague Convention on Choice of Court Agreements (2005):**
- UK ratified post-Brexit
- Netherlands bound via EU accession
- **Exclusive choice of court clauses recognised internationally**
- Judgments from chosen court automatically recognised in signatory states

**Optimal Strategy:**
- **Governing law:** Netherlands (Art. 15i TDM opt-out + strong moral rights)

## 11. Practical Guidance for Palimpsest Implementation

**Cross-Border Enforcement:**

## 12. Conclusion

### 12.1 Optimal Jurisdiction: Netherlands

The Netherlands provides the **strongest legal foundation** for the Palimpsest License:
1. **Moral rights:** Inalienable, comprehensive (Art. 25 Aw)
2. **AI/TDM:** Statutory opt-out mechanism (Art. 15i(3))

### 12.3 UK (England and Wales) as Fallback

England and Wales provide:
- **Developed case law:** Extensive copyright jurisprudence
- **IPEC:** Lower-cost dispute resolution
- **International reputation:** Trusted legal system

**Limitation:** Weaker moral rights (assertion requirement); uncertain commercial TDM exception.

### 12.4 Final Recommendation

**Palimpsest License should:**
1. **Primary jurisdiction:** Netherlands (Dutch law, Dutch courts)
2. **Bilingual framework:** Dutch (legally binding) + English (informative)
3. **Alternative forum:** Scottish courts (if parties prefer English-language proceedings)
4. **Governance structure:** Netherlands *Stichting* for Palimpsest Stewardship Council

This multi-jurisdictional strategy maximises legal protection, cultural alignment, and international enforceability.
